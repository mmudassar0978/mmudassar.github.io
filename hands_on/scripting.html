⚙️ Scripting & Automation in DevOps

Scripting is one of the most important components in modern DevOps practices. It helps engineers automate repetitive tasks and reduce manual effort in system operations. It improves consistency across different environments and reduces the chances of human error. DevOps teams rely on scripting to manage infrastructure and application deployments efficiently. It also plays a key role in monitoring systems and maintaining uptime in production. Through scripting, complex workflows can be simplified into repeatable processes. It enables faster delivery of software by supporting CI/CD pipelines. Automation through scripting improves scalability and reliability of systems. Overall, scripting is a core foundation that connects development and operations in DevOps.

1. Bash Scripting in DevOps

I implemented bash scripting and performed following two task:

Resume Deployment Task

I implemented Bash scripting to automate the deployment of my resume on a local server. The process included pulling my GitHub repository, building a Docker image, and pushing it to Docker Hub. After that, I created a Docker container from the image and exposed it on port 8089. Finally, I accessed my resume through localhost successfully.
